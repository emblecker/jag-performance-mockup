The JAG CS Performance Review spans 220 employees across Home Office functions and restaurant locations from DC to Las Vegas and beyond. 91% have completed their self-appraisals — a strong participation rate. On the reviewer side, however, the picture is more nuanced: only 18 reviews (8%) have been formally submitted per Review Status. An additional 129 reviewers (59%) have entered section scores but not yet hit submit, and 73 employees (33%) have no reviewer input at all.

Among employees with reviewer section data (whether submitted or in progress), the average reviewer score is 3.62 / 5.00 — solidly Advanced. Employees rate themselves higher at 4.00, a 0.38-point perception gap that warrants calibration. The most urgent action is twofold: getting the 129 in-progress reviewers to formally submit, and engaging the 73 reviewers who haven't started at all.

Standout performers — Daniel Moline (4.83), Brett Talbot (4.83), Carlota Andres (4.50) — provide clear recognition anchors. On the other end, several employees with very low reviewer scores (Hayes 2.00, Hsu 1.50, Jimenez 1.33) require urgent follow-up. The most pressing action remains driving 74 outstanding reviewer completions.

Drive review submissions — 59% of reviewers have started but not submitted; 33% haven't begun
▼ Expand

The reviewer completion picture has two distinct problems. First, 129 reviewers (59% of the workforce) have entered section scores but not formally submitted their review — their data is visible but the cycle is not officially closed for those employees. The largest in-progress caseloads belong to Gorsen (15 in progress), Lopez/Nicolas (12), Hsu (9), and Kaplan (9). Second, 73 employees (33%) have received zero reviewer input — their reviewers haven't started at all. The biggest not-started gaps: Grublauskas (8), Gulinello (8), Hsu (7), Ganju (6), Nova (5). Hsu appears on both lists — 9 in progress and 7 not started across her 29-person team.

→ Send escalation reminders to all reviewers with outstanding scores (see template below)

Facilitate cross-reviewer calibration — 0.38 average perception gap requires alignment
▼ Expand

The organization-wide average perception gap (employee self-rating minus reviewer rating) is −0.38 points — calculated from completed section scores only. While some gap is normal, this level is significant enough to warrant calibration before scores are communicated. Additionally, reviewer averages range from 4.83 (Schmitt) to under 1.0 for some reviewers — indicating highly variable rating standards across the organization.

→ Convene a calibration session with all people managers before individual feedback delivery
→ Anchor calibration on rating definitions: Proficient (3.0–3.49), Advanced (3.5–4.49), Role Model (4.5+)
→ Flag reviewers with outlier averages (both high and low) for deeper discussion
→ Use section-level gaps to structure calibration — Leadership & Team Development shows the widest delta (1.51 points)
